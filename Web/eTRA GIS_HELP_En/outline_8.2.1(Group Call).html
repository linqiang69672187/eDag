Group Call

  • Call Toolbar: Initiate Group Call; Receive Group Call

  • Call Panel: Initiate Group Call; Receive Group Call

  • Uplink Call Panel: Receive Group Call; Initiate Group Call

The steps of initiating a group call through call toolbar are as follows:

1) Click the Group Call icon on the call toolbar, and the groups exist in the system appears on the right. Select a group, such as TG4 (10010004), shown in Figure 1.1.

Figure 1.1. Select the group

2) A Microphone icon will pop up beside the call toolbar. Click the Microphone icon to initiate the group call.

3) Hold down the Microphone icon to speak, shown in Figure 1.2. Release the Microphone icon to release the permission.

Figure 1.2. Dispatcher obtains permission

4) After the dispatcher releases the Microphone icon, members in the group can hold down the PTT button to speak, shown in Figure 1.3.

Figure 1.3. Group member obtains permission

5) When the last member in this group call hangs up the terminal, the call ends.

Note:

The dispatcher cannot end the group call initiated through call toolbar.

The steps of receiving a group call through call toolbar are as follows:

1) When the dispatch console receives a group call from a user, such as user 24004, who belongs to group TG4 (10010004), an icon will appear on the side of the user on the map, shown in Figure 1.4.

Figure 1.4. Call access request

2) The dispatch console can receive the voices directly. After the group member releases the PTT button, the call toolbar is shown in Figure 1.5. Then the dispatcher can hold down the Microphone icon to speak.

Figure 1.5. Group member releases permission

3) When the user or the last member in this group call hangs up the terminal, the call ends.

The steps of initiating a group call through call panel are as follows:

1) Click [Dispatch/Call panel] on the menu bar and click "Group call”. The “Group call” dialog box pops up.

2) Enter the group ID, such as 10010004. The system will get the corresponding information automatically. Click <PTT> to initiate the group call.

3) Press <PTT> to speak, shown in Figure 2.1.

Figure 2.1. Dispatcher obtains permission

4) After the dispatcher releases <PTT>, members in the group user can hold down the PTT button to speak, shown in Figure 2.2.

Figure 2.2. Group member obtains permission

6) Click <End> to end the call.

The steps of receiving a group call through call panel are as follows:

1) When the dispatch console receives a group call from the user 24004, who belongs to group TG4 (10010004), the “Group call” dialog box is shown in Figure 2.3.

Figure 2.3. Call access request

2) The dispatch console can receive the voices directly. After the group member releases the PTT button, the call panel is shown in Figure 2.4. Then the dispatcher can press <PTT> to speak.

Figure 2.4. Group member releases permission

3) Click <End> to end the call.

The steps of receiving a group call through uplink call panel are as follows:

1) When the dispatch console receives a group call from a mobile user, such as user 24004, who belongs to group TG4 (10010004), the group call panel is shown in Figure 3.1.

Figure 3.1. Call access request

2) The dispatch console can receive the voices directly. After the group member releases the PTT button, the group call panel is shown in Figure 3.2. Then the dispatcher can press <PTT> to speak.

Figure 3.2. Group member releases permission

3) When the user 24004 or the last member in this group call hangs up the terminal, the call ends.

The steps of initiating a group call through uplink call panel are as follows:

1) Click <PTT> in the group call panel to initiate the call.

2) Press <PTT> to speak, shown in Figure 3.3.

Figure 3.3. Dispatcher obtains permission

3) After the dispatcher releases <PTT>, members in the group can hold down the PTT button to preempt the transmission permission.

4) When the last member in this group call hangs up the terminal, the call ends.

Note:

In the uplink call panel, you can only initiate a group call to the terminal that already has the group call records.
