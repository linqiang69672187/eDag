Ambience Listening

When a mobile user is idle, the dispatcher can monitor the user’s surrounding environment without being noticed.

Once the ambience listening is set up, the monitored user cannot cancel the monitoring or receive other calls, except for the emergency call. The monitored terminal will automatically open its microphone to collect the surrounding voices, and transmit the voice packet to the dispatch console.

However, if the monitored user initiates a call or receives an emergency call, the ambience listening will end automatically.

The steps of ambience listening are as follows:

1) Click [Dispatch/Call panel] on the menu bar and click “Ambience listening”. The “Ambience listening” dialog box pops up.

2) Enter the terminal ID, such as 180008. The system will get the corresponding information automatically, shown in Figure 1.

Figure 1. “Ambience listening” dialog box

3) Click <Start>. Then the dispatcher can receive the voices around the user, shown in Figure 2.

Figure 2. Start ambience listening

4) Click <End> to end the ambience listening, shown in Figure 3.

Figure 3. End the ambience listening

5) During the monitoring process, if the user initiates a call or receives an emergency call, the ambience listening will end, shown in Figure 4.

Figure 4. Ambience listening is interrupted

Note:

  • At a time, the dispatch console could only monitor one mobile user.

  • If a mobile user is not in the system, turned off or in a call, it will fail to monitor the user.
